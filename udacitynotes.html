Important Concepts

The World Wide Web, How it Works, and HTML:

Components of the web include: the user, you and your computer running a web browser such as Chrome (which is what I prefer), Safari, Firefox, and Internet Explorer, the Internet, and servers (computers like yours, but instead of browsing files, they are optimized for hosting files).

HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language and it is the basis, or foundation, for almost every webpage; it can include documents, images, videos, etc. HTML is the glue of connectivity that creates the "web" in which webpages are connected, or linked, by hyperlinks, making the web, "weblike." I was surprised to learn that there are some 30 billion pages on world wide web. Talk about a web that could be strung, with so many pages linking to each other.

Furthermore, HTML is made up of:
  • text content, or what you see
  • markup, or what it looks like
  • linkes to other pages, or hyperlinks
  • references to other documents, such as images, videos, etc.
